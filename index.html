Basis
The difference between a buyer's local cash price and the CBOT futures price. A negative basis means the buyer pays that amount under futures. A tighter (less negative) basis means a stronger local market.
Cash Price
Buyers with a date/time tag show the actual posted cash price pulled directly from the elevator's website. Buyers without a tag show an estimated cash price calculated from CBOT futures + an estimated basis.
Date/Time Tags
A green tag means the cash price and basis were pulled from the elevator's posted bids within the last 24 hours. A red tag means the data is more than 24 hours old and may not reflect current pricing. Buyers with no tag use estimated values. Always confirm with your buyer before delivering.

ARC / PLC Program Context
The USDA Agriculture Risk Coverage (ARC) and Price Loss Coverage (PLC) programs are the grain safety net, enrolled through your local FSA office. PLC pays when the national market year average falls below the reference price — corn: $3.70/bu, soybeans: $8.40/bu. ARC-CO pays when actual county revenue falls below 86% of the Olympic average benchmark. Use this calculator to know your break-even, then compare it against the PLC reference price to gauge your exposure.

LRP Insurance Context
USDA Livestock Risk Protection (LRP) is a federally subsidized price insurance program for feeder and fed cattle. It protects against a price decline between purchase and your target end date — USDA covers roughly 35% of the premium. Coverage is available through licensed crop insurance agents with no herd size minimum. Use this calculator to find your break-even sell price, then compare it to available LRP coverage levels to decide if downside protection makes sense.

How to Read This Dashboard
Minnesota Milk Classes
Class IFluid milk — highest sanitation standard. Commands a premium over Class III. Estimated as CME Class III + USDA Order 30 Class I differential (~$2.00–$3.50/cwt).
Class IIIManufacturing milk — cheese, butter, powder. Most southern MN dairy farmers ship Class III. Price tracks CME Class III futures directly.
Your Mailbox Price
Order 30 blend + your plant's premium = estimated mailbox price. The premium covers hauling, quality bonuses, and co-op deductions. Verify monthly with your plant or fieldman.
DMC Program
Dairy Margin Coverage uses the Class III price. If it drops below your coverage level, DMC pays the difference. See the Margin Calc tab.

DMC Program Context
The USDA Dairy Margin Coverage (DMC) program pays when the national margin (Class III average minus DMC feed cost index) falls below your coverage level. Coverage levels range from $4.00–$9.50/cwt. This calculator shows your actual margin based on your operation's costs — use it alongside DMC to see if you're covered.

The Platform Why It's Free Built with AI Data Sources
grow27 started with a simple idea — the same intelligence and automation tools available to large agricultural operations should be accessible to every farm, regardless of size.
Originally engineered for southern Minnesota operations —with a special thanks to #27, DFM, & MFM— grow27 was built to scale beyond a single farm. Our goal is to empower every operation with the same advanced tools, real-time data, and automated insights, bridging the gap between spend and capability.
The Platform
grow27 is the platform. #27Markets is the first piece of it —watching the boards so you don't have to— with live grain and cattle market data, local buyer prices, and margin calculators built for working farmers. #27Herd, #27Fields, and #27Finance are coming, each adding another layer of intelligence to the operation.
Why It's Free
grow27 is hosted directly on GitHub Pages and doesn't store any personal information. There are no accounts, no tracking, and no data leaving your device. Because we don't hold your data, we don't charge for access. Everything runs directly in your browser.
Future versions may offer optional data storage — contract tracking, herd records, field history — but that will always be your choice.
Built with AI
grow27 was designed and built using AI-assisted development, proving that powerful, professional tools don't require a large team or budget to build. That's the point.
